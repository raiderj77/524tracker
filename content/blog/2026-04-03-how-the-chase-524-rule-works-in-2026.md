# How the Chase 5/24 Rule Works in 2026
=================================================================

The Chase 5/24 Rule is a critical consideration for anyone looking to apply for a Chase credit card in 2026. To answer the question straight away: **if you've opened 5 or more new credit card accounts in the past 24 months, you may be excluded from earning sign-up bonuses with many Chase credit cards**.

### What is the Chase 5/24 Rule?

The Chase 5/24 Rule is a policy applied by Chase Bank to control the number of credit cards you can earn sign-up bonuses on. Introduced in 2016, this rule is designed to prevent customers from gaming the system by opening multiple credit cards in a short period.

### How Does the Rule Work?

In simple terms, the Chase 5/24 Rule blocks you from earning sign-up bonuses on new credit card accounts if you've opened 5 or more new credit card accounts in the past 24 months. This includes:

* New credit card accounts for personal or business cards
* Sign-up bonuses, in addition to existing credit card income (revolving credit and income rewards)
* Credit card applications that are approved, and not pending

### Credit Card Accounts Excluded from the Rule

Certain types of credit card accounts are excluded from the Chase 5/24 Rule:

* Credit cards from other banks, such as American Express or Citi
* Secured credit cards or business loan applications
* Personal loan, mortgages, and other Chase loan products
* Chase Ink for Business (Business cards) are counted separately for the 5/24 rule.

### Credit Card Accounts Included in the Rule

The following types of credit card accounts are included in the Chase 5/24 Rule:

* Chase Freedom Flex
* Chase Freedom Unlimited
* Chase Sapphire Preferred
* Chase Sapphire Reserve
* Chase Ink Cash Business
* Chase Ink Preferred Business 
* Chase Sapphire
### How Do You Check Your Credit Card Accounts?

To see how many credit card accounts you've opened in the past 24 months, you can:

* Use a credit card tracking tool, like Credit Karma or Credit Sesame
* Check your Chase account history online, or through the Chase app
* Review your credit report from the three major credit bureaus: Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion

### Ways to Get Around the Rule

If you're eager to earn sign-up bonuses on multiple Chase credit cards, consider the following strategies:

* Look for credit cards that don't count towards the 5/24 rule, like those from other banks
* Open a credit card account and close it within the same 30-day cycle to avoid the count
* Consider applying for a personal loan or other Chase loan product
* Space out your credit card applications to avoid maxing out the 5/24 limit

### Can This Rule Change in the Future?

The Chase 5/24 Rule may be revised or changed at any time, making it essential to stay informed about any updates. With new credit card releases, and changes in the market, this rule serves as a reminder to maintain responsible credit card habits.

### Important Considerations

Bear in mind:

* The Chase 5/24 Rule is subject to change
* Different Chase credit cards may have varying sign-up bonuses and restrictions
* Always review the terms and conditions of credit card applications before applying
### How the Chase 5/24 Rule Affects You

Understanding the Chase 5/24 Rule allows you to make informed decisions about your credit card applications. Take time to track your applications and sign-up bonuses to maximize the rewards from Chase credit cards.
