# How the Chase 5/24 Rule Works in 2026
=====================================================

Are you a credit card enthusiast looking to maximize your rewards earning potential on 524tracker.com? Here are the first two sentences that you need to know: the Chase 5/24 rule dictates that applicants cannot be approved for most Chase credit cards if they have opened five or more new credit card accounts in the past 24 months.

**Understanding the Chase 5/24 Rule**
--------------------------------------

The Chase 5/24 rule is a simple yet crucial policy for credit card applicants. To apply for a Chase credit card and be approved, the applicant can have a maximum of four new credit inquiries in the last 24 months. Once you've exceeded this limit, you're unlikely to be approved for most Chase credit cards.

### Benefits of the Chase 5/24 Rule

While some may view the Chase 5/24 rule as a restriction, it actually serves as a safeguard for credit card applicants. By limiting the number of new credit inquiries, Chase aims to:

* Reduce the risk of credit card applicants taking on too much debt
* Ensure credit card applicants are making responsible financial decisions
* Protect applicants from overspending and falling into financial difficulty

### Exceptions to the Chase 5/24 Rule

While the Chase 5/24 rule applies to most Chase credit cards, there are some exceptions. Applicants who have received a credit limit increase, sign-up bonus, or credit card upgrade in the last 24 months can proceed with a new application. However, it's essential to note that these exceptions may have specific requirements and are not guaranteed.
