# How to Track Your Credit Card Application History
====================================================

As a savvy credit cardholder, you're likely no stranger to applying for new cards in pursuit of better rewards, higher credit limits, or exclusive perks. But have you ever wondered how to keep tabs on your past credit card applications – including the ones that got rejected? In this comprehensive guide, we'll show you exactly how to track your credit card application history

## Importance of Tracking Your Credit Card Application History

### Why You Should Care

*   Your credit card application history can impact your credit score
*   It may affect your chances of getting approved for future credit card applications
*   You can use this information to spot potential errors or discrepancies on your credit reports

## How Credit Bureaus Store Credit Card Application Data

### What Credit Reporting Agencies Consider

*   Hard inquiries (when a lender checks your credit) can significantly lower your credit score short-term
*   Soft inquiries (e.g., when you check your own credit report) have zero impact on credit scores
*   Credit scoring models factor hard inquiries into the approval or denial process

## Tracking Credit Card Applications with the Major Credit Bureaus

### How to Access Your Credit Reports

*   You can contact the three major credit bureaus and request a copy of your report for free once a year
*   You can also access your credit reports online through various services and websites
*   Use this information to review your history of applications, approvals, and rejections

### Reviewing Your Credit Report

*   Make sure your list of past credit card applications accurately reflects your actual history
*   Spot any potential errors that might have affected your credit score
*   Note down the dates and results of your past applications

## Online Tools for Tracking Credit Card Applications

### Browser Extensions and Software

*   Browser extensions like Credit Karma and Credit Score Assistant make it easy to monitor your credit report
*   Credit monitoring software (e.g., Experian, Equifax) can offer real-time alerts about new inquiries and credit changes

### How to Choose the Right Tools

*   Research and compare the features and limitations of different browser extensions and software
*   Consider your personal preferences, credit goals, and budget when selecting a tracking tool

## Staying Organized and Informed

### Keeping Records

*   Document your credit card applications, including the date, lender, and outcome
*   Use a spreadsheet or note-taking app to stay organized and easily reference past applications
*   Update your records as new applications and inquiries appear

### Credit Card Application Timeline

*   Start a table or calendar to keep track of your past credit card applications
*   List the lender, date, status, and any relevant notes or comments
*   Review your timeline regularly to identify trends and areas for improvement

## FAQ

### Q: How often can I check my own credit report?

A: You can access your credit report from the major credit bureaus once a year for free.

### Q: What information do credit scores factor in?

A: Hard inquiries, credit utilization, payment history, and credit age contribute to your credit score.

### Q: How long does a hard inquiry stay on my credit report?

A: A hard inquiry typically stays on your credit report for two years.

### Q: What happens if I notice an error on my credit report?

A: Dispute the error directly with the credit bureau, and they will investigate the claim.
