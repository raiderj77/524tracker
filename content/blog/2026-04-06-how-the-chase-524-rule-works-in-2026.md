## Understanding the Chase 5/24 Rule
### What is the Chase 5/24 Rule?

The Chase 5/24 rule is a set of guidelines that dictates the eligibility requirements for applicants seeking to obtain a Chase-branded credit card. The rule is straightforward: if you've had more than five new credit accounts opened in the past 24 months, you will be denied approval for most Chase credit cards.

### The Key Components of the Rule

There are a few key components to the Chase 5/24 rule that you should understand.

*   **New credit accounts**: Only new credit accounts opened in the past 24 months are counted, not existing accounts or accounts that have been opened for more than two years.
*   **More than five accounts**: The rule specifically states that applicants with more than five new credit accounts in the past 24 months will be denied approval.
*   **Credit cards**: This rule applies only to credit cards issued by Chase, not to other types of credit or financial products.
