# How Hard Inquiries Affect Your Credit Score
==============================================

Do hard inquiries on your credit report automatically lower your credit score? The answer is yes, but there's more to it than just that one word.

Hard inquiries do affect your credit score, but the impact is often temporary and can be mitigated with good credit habits.

**What Are Hard Inquiries?**
---------------------------

Hard inquiries occur when you apply for credit and the lender checks your credit report to determine your creditworthiness. This results in a temporary drop in credit score, which can last anywhere from a few months to a few years. The reason for this is that hard inquiries indicate to lenders that you're taking on more debt, which may increase the risk of default.

**How Do Hard Inquiries Affect Credit Scores?**
-------------------------------------------

When you apply for credit, the lender will perform a hard inquiry, which can cause your credit score to drop temporarily. The amount of the drop will depend on the score and the number of hard inquiries on your report.

### Factors Affecting Hard Inquiry Impact

*   **Credit Score Type**: The impact of hard inquiries is more significant on credit scores like FICO, which are commonly used for credit card approvals and other types of lending.
*   **Number of Hard Inquiries**: The more hard inquiries on your report, the more significant the impact on your credit score.
*   **Timeframe**: The longer the time between hard inquiries, the less of an impact on your credit score.

### Examples of Hard Inquiries

*   Applying for a credit card
*   Applying for a mortgage or auto loan
*   Applying for a personal loan or credit line
*   Opening a new credit account

**How Long Do Hard Inquiries Stay on Your Credit Report?**
--------------------------------------------------------

Hard inquiries remain on your credit report for up to 2 years. However, their impact on your credit score is typically temporary, lasting anywhere from a few months to a year.

### How Long Do Hard Inquiries Affect Credit Scores?

The impact of hard inquiries on credit scores is usually temporary, lasting anywhere from a few months to a year. The longer the time since the hard inquiry, the less of an impact on your credit score.

### Examples of Credit Score Impact After Hard Inquiries

*   10-30 points drop after 1-3 months
*   5-15 points drop after 6-12 months
*   0-5 points drop after 1-2 years

**How to Mitigate the Impact of Hard Inquiries**
----------------------------------------------

While hard inquiries do affect your credit score, there are ways to mitigate their impact.

### Maintaining Good Credit Habits

*   Make on-time payments
*   Keep credit utilization low
*   Monitor credit report for errors

### Avoiding Multiple Hard Inquiries

*   Space out credit applications
*   Use pre-approval for credit cards and loans

**FAQs**
----

### Q: Can I Remove Hard Inquiries from My Credit Report?

A: No, hard inquiries cannot be removed from your credit report. However, their impact on your credit score is usually temporary.

### Q: Will Closing Old Accounts Remove Hard Inquiries?

A: Closing old accounts may not remove hard inquiries, but it can help reduce the impact on your credit score.

### Q: Can I Get a Credit Score With No Hard Inquiries?

A: Yes, credit scores from credit reporting agencies like Experian, TransUnion, or Equifax often don't include a history of credit inquiries.
